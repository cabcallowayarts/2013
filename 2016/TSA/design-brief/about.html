ABOUT

The first drone recorded in history was in France, September 16th, 1782. The Montgolfier brothers were the first to experiment with balloons. They first tested these blimps with unmanned vehicles.

Most standard drones consist of flight controllers and GPS integration, multirotor (revolving blade), a battery, and a lightweight frame, usually made of a carbon fiber. Rotors that have geometric blades and are distributed around the drone in geometric shape, like an octagon. This reduces failure and increases balance. Enhancement in building components such as lighter materials and faster rotors can lead to the development of passenger drones. With smarter and more effective designs, safety can be enhanced and fail-safe systems can be implemented.

  • MICRO UAV

    Micro UAVs are commercially available drones operated with remote controls. They have small wingspans and are extremely light weight. One of the smallest Micro UAVs has a 6 inch wingspan and was developed by DARPA. Stanford University is developing a micro UAV with a diameter of 1 cm.

  • BIOMETRIC UAV

    Biomimetic UAVs are drones which resemble elements from nature. Most notably in drone design, Biomimetic designs are usually birds. These unconventional designs could prove useful in multiple ways. Nature photographers could use these UAVs to get a closer look at animals. This aircraft could also provide new surveillance opportunities in urban environments.

  • BLIMPS/BALLOONS

    Blimps for commercial use are now in development. They provide a wide range of advantages compared to other unmanned aircraft vehicles. They could be used for the surveillance of bridges, roadways, and assist in natural disasters. The blimp design allows them to hover high above the ground for extended period of times.

COMMERCIAL

UAVs have grown in popularity over the past several years, attracting the eyes of businesses, photographers, and other entrepreneurs.

EDUCATIONAL

UAV's are not only useful in commercial settings, but can also be useful in education, from training students how to use drones, to using drones to learn robotics.

RESEARCH

The ability of a drone to maneuver quickly and nimbly allows it to be used very effectively in research, as it can get places that humans cannot with relative ease.
