Recreational uses of augmented reality have become increasingly popular as the popular smartphone application Pokemon Go has over 21 million daily users in the US. The public are able to interact with their environment with the new developments in gaming, art, and overall entertainment.

pokemon go

Pokemon Go has become a popular application available for download on smartphones that uses augmented reality to allow users to interact with their surrounding environment and “catch” pokemon wherever they go.

tilt brush

Tilt brush is a tool developed by Google that allows artists to 3D paint their surroundings. Tilt brush application is designed for motion interfaces in virtual reality but also works with keyboard and mouse. Finished work can export their images as animated GIFs.

magic leap

Magic Leap is a company that is developing a technology to overlay three-dimensional computer generated images over real world objects. While the company has not released a product, it invites the public to the possibilities that there are with this technology.

hololens

Microsoft HoloLens is a self-contained, holographic computer that enables you to engage with your digital content and interact with holograms in the world around you that is built into a headset. It essentially lets you see, hear, and interact with holograms within an environment such as a living room or an office space

who's watching?

Concerns in recreational uses arise in the question of privacy. Augmented reality records and analyzes data from the environment in real time. While the first amendment of the Constitution allows for recording for public interest, legal complications occur when recording outside the public domain or copyrighted media is displayed.
